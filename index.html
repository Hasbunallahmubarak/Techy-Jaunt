Nollywood, Nigeria’s vibrant and prolific film industry, has been on a remarkable journey over the years. Started late 20th century, now a top global film industry with 2,500+ annual productions.

Nollywood excels in storytelling, characters, and addressing social issues, gaining global recognition for both quality and quantity in film production. In 2023, Nollywood promises global audiences diverse, exciting films, maintaining its reputation for thrilling and captivating cinematic experiences. Let’s take a closer look at some of the most anticipated Nollywood movies of the year.

1. The Trade (Genre: Drama)

“The Trade” promises to be a gripping drama that explores the harrowing world of human trafficking. The film revolves around two women from entirely different backgrounds who are brought together by a shared tragedy: their children are kidnapped and sold into the sex trade.

Their journey is bound to be heart-wrenching, as it sheds light on an issue that plagues society, all the while highlighting the resilience and strength of the human spirit.

2. Love in a Pandemic (Genre: Romantic Comedy)

“Love in a Pandemic” thoughtfully delves into the myriad challenges of romance during the COVID-19 pandemic. It’s a romantic comedy that explores how two young individuals navigate the trials and tribulations of love in a time of social distancing and uncertainty.

With humor and heart, this film offers a fresh perspective on the universal theme of love conquering all obstacles.

3. Gangs of Lagos (Genre: Crime Thriller)

The blockbuster movie, “Gangs of Lagos” will thrillingly transport viewers into the gritty and perilous world of organized crime in Lagos, Nigeria.

The film follows the lives of several gang members vying for power and control. With its suspenseful narrative, complex characters, and an exploration of the underbelly of urban life, this crime thriller is likely to captivate audiences.

Since it’s debut, “Shanty Town” has offered a poignant look into sex workers’ lives in Lagos slum, highlighting their struggle for survival.

5. Orisa(Genre: Epic Fantasy)

“Orisa” delves into the rich world of Yoruba mythology and gods. Epic fantasy film depicts deities in human world, providing visually stunning, spiritually resonant experience.

When a king becomes rogue and loses respect for the gods and the people, insanity becomes his fate. This predicament befalls him at a time when a terrorist, who he once struck a deal with, returns to forcefully take over the kingdom.

The Nigerian crime thriller film, “The Black Book,” follows a professor whose family faces kidnapping and murder.

Dark October, a Nigerian drama film, tells the true story of four university students who fell victim to a mob attack.
